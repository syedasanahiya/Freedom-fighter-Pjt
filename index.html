Rani Lakshmi Bai

Rani Lakshmi Bai, the Queen of Jhansi, was one of the bravest freedom fighters of India. Born in 1828, she became a symbol of courage and resistance during the First War of Independence in 1857. Known for her leadership, she fought fearlessly against the British, inspiring her army and the nation with her words and actions. Her famous spirit lives on in the saying, “I shall not give up my Jhansi.” Rani Lakshmi Bai remains a timeless icon of bravery and patriotism.

Indira Gandhi

Indira Gandhi, the first woman Prime Minister of India, served the nation with strong leadership and determination. Born in 1917, she became known for her bold decisions, including the Green Revolution, which transformed India’s agriculture. Her famous words, “My life is dedicated to the service of my people,” reflect her commitment to the nation. Indira Gandhi remains one of India’s most influential leaders, remembered for her courage and vision.
